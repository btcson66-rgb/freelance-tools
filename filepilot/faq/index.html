FilePilot Local FAQ

Answers about offline processing, OCR, Taiwan documents, pricing and Windows support. Need help? Email support.

Basics

What is FilePilot Local?

FilePilot Local is an offline Windows desktop app for renaming and filing messy documents such as scanned PDFs, invoices, receipts, contracts, quotes, bank documents, images, DOCX, XLSX, CSV and TXT files.

Who is it for?

It is for people who already have folders full of business documents and want useful filenames and category folders without uploading those documents to a cloud service.

Which Windows versions are supported?

Windows 10 and Windows 11 64-bit are supported. FilePilot Local is available as an installer and as a portable exe.

Is the interface English only?

No. The UI is switchable between English and Traditional Chinese.

Privacy and safety

Does FilePilot Local upload files or use cloud AI?

No. FilePilot Local processes documents on your computer. It does not upload your files and does not use a cloud AI API.

Does it ever auto-delete files?

No. FilePilot Local never auto-deletes documents.

How are duplicates handled?

Duplicates are flagged to a Duplicates folder instead of being deleted.

What happens when extraction confidence is low?

Low-confidence files go to Needs Review so you can check them manually before deciding where they belong.

Can I undo a rename or move?

Every rename and move is logged. Pro adds batch undo for reversing a whole run at once.

Files and extraction

Which file types are supported?

Supported inputs include scanned PDFs, regular PDFs, invoices, receipts, contracts, quotes, bank documents, images with OCR, DOCX, XLSX, CSV and TXT.

Which OCR languages are supported?

OCR supports Traditional Chinese and English for scanned PDFs and photos.

What information does FilePilot Local extract?

It extracts the date, company, amount and document type, then uses those fields for filename templates and category folders.

How do filename templates work?

Templates use extracted fields such as {date}, {company}, {documentType} and {amount}. A common pattern is {date}_{company}_{documentType}_{amount}.

Which folders can it file documents into?

It can file documents into practical category folders such as Invoices, Receipts, Contracts, Quotes and Bank.

Does it support Taiwan document formats?

Yes. FilePilot Local understands ROC dates, NT$ amounts, Chinese-numeral amounts, 8-digit Taiwan tax IDs with checksum and Taiwan invoice numbers, while also supporting normal English documents.
