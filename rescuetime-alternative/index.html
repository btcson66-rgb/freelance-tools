Why freelancers look past RescueTime

RescueTime is a capable automatic productivity tracker, but for a solo freelancer it can be more than the job needs: an account, a web app, and a recurring plan to do what is, at its core, knowing where your billable time goes.

TimeTrack takes the opposite bet. It is a single file that runs offline, keeps every second of your tracked time on your own device, and never asks you to log in or subscribe. You trade team features you may not use for privacy, simplicity, and ownership.

Frequently asked questions

Is TimeTrack really free?
The core tool is free to use with no account. Pro adds extra features as a one-time purchase, not a subscription.
Does it work offline like RescueTime doesn't?
Yes. TimeTrack runs entirely on your device with no internet required, whereas RescueTime is a cloud service.
Can it replace RescueTime for a team?
Not really. TimeTrack is built for solo freelancers. If you need team collaboration and a full platform, RescueTime is the better fit.
Where is my data stored?
On your own machine, in a file you own. It never goes to a vendor's servers.
Do I lose my data if I stop paying?
No. Because the file is local and yours, there is no subscription that can lock you out of your own records.
