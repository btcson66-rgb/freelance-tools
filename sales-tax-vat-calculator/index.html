Add tax, or work it backwards — both in one tool

Two tax questions come up constantly on invoices. The first is forward: you have a net price and need to add tax to it. The second is reverse: you have a total that already includes tax and need to know how much of it is tax — the classic "remove VAT" calculation. Doing the reverse one in your head is where mistakes happen, because you cannot just subtract the percentage; you have to divide. This calculator handles both cleanly.

In add-tax mode, enter the net amount and a rate and it shows the tax and the gross total. In remove-tax mode, enter a tax-inclusive total and it shows the net amount and the exact tax inside it. Either way it displays the net, the tax and the gross together, so the numbers on your invoice are never ambiguous.

Frequently asked questions

Is this calculator free?
Yes, completely. It runs in your browser with no account and nothing uploaded. There is no paid version of the calculator itself.
How do I add tax to a price?
Multiply the net by the rate for the tax, then add it. $100 net at 20% = $20 tax, $120 gross. The tool shows all three.
How do I remove VAT from a total?
Divide the inclusive total by (1 + rate) for the net, then subtract for the tax. $120 at 20% = $100 net, $20 tax. Use remove-tax mode.
What rate should I use?
It depends on your country, registration status and product. The tool pre-fills example rates but every value is editable. It's a helper, not tax advice.
Is my data private?
Completely. Zero network requests — your amounts stay on your computer.
