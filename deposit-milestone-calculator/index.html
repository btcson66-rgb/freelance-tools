A deposit protects you; milestones keep you paid

Starting a project on a promise is how freelancers end up chasing money. A deposit up front confirms the client is serious and pays for your early work, and splitting the rest into milestone payments means you are never carrying weeks of unpaid work at once. The only friction is the maths — and doing it in your head while quoting is how mistakes creep in.

This calculator removes that friction. Put in the project total, pick two, three or four payments, and tap a quick-split preset (50/50, 40/30/30, even) or type your own percentages. It shows the exact amount for each payment, highlights the deposit to request first, and flags it immediately if your percentages don't add up to 100%.

Frequently asked questions

Is this deposit calculator free?
Yes, completely. It runs in your browser with no account and nothing uploaded. There is no paid version of the calculator itself.
How much deposit should a freelancer ask for?
30–50% of the project total is common — it confirms commitment and covers early work. For larger projects, split the balance into milestones to reduce risk further.
What is a milestone payment?
A portion of the fee due when a defined stage is reached (deposit on signing, payment at the halfway point, final on delivery), which protects your cash flow on longer projects.
How do I split a project into payments?
Enter the total, choose how many payments, and set each percentage. Common splits are 50/50 or 40/30/30. The tool shows each amount and checks the split sums to 100%.
Is my data private?
Completely. Zero network requests — your project values stay on your computer.
