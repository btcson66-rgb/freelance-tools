Why freelancers look past Timely

Timely's selling point is automatic time tracking — it records what you do across apps and uses AI to draft your timesheet. Powerful for some, but it means a memory of your activity sits in the cloud, behind an account and a subscription.

TimeTrack is deliberately manual and local: you start a timer, you stop it, and the record stays on your device. No background monitoring, no AI, no upload. If you want control over what is recorded and where it lives, the manual approach is a feature, not a limitation.

Frequently asked questions

Does TimeTrack track my activity automatically?
No. It's a manual one-click timer by design. Only what you choose to time is recorded, unlike Timely's automatic background tracking.
Is my data private?
Yes. Everything stays in a local file on your device. Nothing is monitored or uploaded to a server.
Is it free?
The core timer is free with no account. Pro is a one-time purchase, not a subscription.
What if I forget to start the timer?
That's the trade-off of a manual tool. If you frequently forget, Timely's automatic capture may suit you better.
Does it work offline?
Yes, fully. No internet connection is required at any point.
