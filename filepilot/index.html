What FilePilot Local actually does

Reads common document types

PDF text extraction for normal PDFs, OCR for scanned PDFs and photos, plus DOCX, XLSX, CSV and TXT support.

Extracts filing fields

Finds the date, company, amount and document type, then uses those fields in templates like {date}_{company}_{documentType}_{amount}.

Files into category folders

Sorts documents into practical folders such as Invoices, Receipts, Contracts, Quotes and Bank.

Handles scans and photos

OCR works for Traditional Chinese and English, so scanned receipts and phone photos are not treated as blank files.

Taiwan-aware extraction

Understands ROC dates, NT$ amounts, Chinese-numeral amounts, 8-digit tax IDs with checksum, invoice numbers and regular English documents.

English and Traditional Chinese UI

The interface is switchable between English and Traditional Chinese. Runs on Windows 10/11 64-bit with installer and portable exe options.

The workflow

  1. Choose messy files or a folder.
    Use real documents: PDFs, scans, receipts, invoices, images and office files.
  2. FilePilot reads them locally.
    Text extraction and OCR run on your Windows computer. No document upload and no cloud AI API.
  3. It extracts the fields.
    Date, company, amount and document type become the data used for names and folders.
  4. It renames and files safely.
    Duplicates are flagged to a Duplicates folder. Low-confidence files go to Needs Review. Files are never auto-deleted.

FAQ

Does FilePilot Local upload my files?

No. Processing runs locally on your Windows computer. FilePilot Local does not upload your documents and does not call a cloud AI API.

Which OCR languages are supported?

OCR supports Traditional Chinese and English, which covers scanned Taiwan receipts and invoices as well as regular English business documents.

What happens to uncertain files?

Low-confidence files go to Needs Review. The goal is to keep uncertain documents visible instead of pretending every extraction is perfect.

Does it delete duplicates?

No. Duplicates are flagged to a Duplicates folder. FilePilot Local never auto-deletes documents.
